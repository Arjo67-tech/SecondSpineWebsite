what is our
mission?

Books are often discarded because their covers are torn, outdated, or damaged — even when the stories inside are still worth reading.

Second Spine gives those books a renewed life by restoring their covers by hand and returning them to places where they can be read again — libraries, classrooms, and community spaces.

free of charge, always

a “second spine” — quite literally.

  • Reduce Waste

    A torn cover is not a reason to throw a book away. We repair what would otherwise be discarded.

  • Restore Books

    New covers, new spines, bound by hand at volunteer workshops. No two come out alike.

  • Share Stories

    Restored books go back to libraries, classrooms, and neighborhoods — free to whoever needs them.

chapter 02

How a book gets its second spine.

Four steps, all by hand. Pull a book off the shelf to read each one.

  1. step one

    Collect

    We gather used, damaged, or unwanted books from families, schools, and community partners. Drop-off boxes live at the Saratoga Library.

  2. step two

    Sort

    Books are checked for readability, condition, age level, and donation fit. Good pages go forward; the rest is recycled responsibly.

  3. step three

    Restore

    Volunteers remove damaged covers, create new covers and spines, and prepare each book for reuse. This is the “second spine.”

  4. step four

    Donate

    Finished books are donated to libraries, classrooms, and community spaces — free to anyone who wants to read them.

chapter 04

Built by students.
Shared with readers.

Second Spine began as a hands-on service project: a way to combine design, sustainability, and literacy into something libraries and students could actually use.

Covers tear. Spines crack. Libraries and schools pull books from their shelves — not because the stories stopped mattering, but because the bindings gave out. We collect those books, rebuild their covers at volunteer workshops, and put them back where readers can find them.

Currently partnering with the Saratoga Library and local schools to collect, restore, and redistribute books to community members who need them.

started in saratoga, ca — growing one shelf at a time
